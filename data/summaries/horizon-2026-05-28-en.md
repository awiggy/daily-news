## [Huawei's τ Law: A New Semiconductor Scaling Paradigm](https://news.google.com/rss/articles/CBMif0FVX3lxTE1pcnAxU2ptVGpBREVHVG9lbnZMU2xrN3lyTE83ZVJyajFmd1dIWlpzZHVXSWdmWHpHTGhxTnpKeDFfNWhZSWVtZmFPZUtUeDMzbHNTbDdORzFweVhkNTJSUlAwb3h2Wi12T2FaazdEUUdBXzhEd3l1X0dXdF9YUGM?oc=5) ⭐️ 6.0/10

Huawei unveiled the τ (Tau) Scaling Law on March 25, 2026, proposing a multi-level co-optimization approach from devices to systems to continue chip density scaling beyond traditional Moore's Law. This law could reshape the semiconductor industry by offering an alternative path to advanced nodes without relying solely on extreme ultraviolet lithography, potentially reducing dependence on ASML's equipment and altering global chip supply chains. Huawei claims to have designed and mass-produced 381 chips based on τ Law principles over the past six years. The law is essentially an evolution of the STCO (System Technology Co-Optimization) methodology, optimizing transistors, interconnects, circuits, and systems holistically.

rss · Google News - 半导体 · May 28, 06:55

**Background**: Moore's Law, which predicts transistor density doubling every two years, has been slowing due to physical limits. Dennard scaling, which enabled voltage and power reduction with each node, also broke down around 2006. The τ Law aims to continue performance gains through system-level co-design rather than pure lithography scaling.
